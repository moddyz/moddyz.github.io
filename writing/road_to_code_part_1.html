Road to Code Part 1: Aspiring Concept Artist

November 22, 2025

I want to share my journey towards writing code professionally. It was not an optimal recommended path.

Did you know what you wanted to do out of high school? I certainly did not.

I was off to a strong start in Grade 9 academically. My parents - highly educated Chinese immigrants - demanded excellence, and for a while, I delivered. Good grades, math contests, the works. The future felt full of possibility.

Grade 10 hit, and some personal issues knocked me sideways. Grade 11 was worse: my mom banned me from playing basketball - the one thing keeping me sane - and I spiraled - I consciously let my grades tank almost as a way to get back at her. C's on the report card became the norm. I immersed into gaming - Defense of the Ancients and Starsiege Tribes weren't just hobbies anymore.

By the time Grade 12 arrived, I watched most of my friends and classmates settle on their universities and majors. I didn't want to end up homeless so had to pick somethihng.

What about Art? That was something. My parents had enrolled me in traditional drawing classes on weekends throughout my childhood which I enjoyed. I was back at it.

Then, I stumbed upon Craig Mullins' website goodbrush.com (now lost to internet history). Loose, atmospheric, impossibly skilled. Here was someone painting sci-fi mechs and fantasy landscapes with the same confidence traditional painters brought to bowls of fruit. It was exciting to know that someoneone making a living creating imaginary worlds.

The decision was made. I wanted to become a concept artist. Should be easy, right?
